<div><span style="font-size: 12px;"><span style="font-family: Times New Roman,Times,serif,-webkit-standard;"><strong><u>APPLICABLE ORDINANCE<br></u></strong><br>BE IT ORDAINED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF SPRINGFIELD, AS FOLLOWS:<br>Section 5 of Chapter 322, Throwing snow and ice into the streets, of the Code of the City of Springfield, is hereby amended by deleting the section in its entirety and inserting the following:<br><br>A. &nbsp; &nbsp;No person, including but not limited to paid or unpaid contractors, property owners, or tenants, shall throw or put, or cause to be thrown or put, any snow or ice into any street in the City or upon any public way. This section shall apply at all times and is not restricted to specific periods following snow events.<br><br>B. &nbsp; &nbsp; This section shall not apply if the Building Commissioner orders the removal of snow or ice from structures adjacent to the public way and a permit is obtained from the Department of Public Works under the provisions of Chapter 369 of the Acts of 1911.<br><br>C. &nbsp; &nbsp; Any person who removes snow or ice and places the snow or ice around or over fire hydrants, into snow piles that result in dangerous driving, biking or pedestrian sight distance issues, in a manner that narrows on-street parking or drive lanes, or in any other manner that creates a hazard to the general public will be in violation of this section.<br><br>D. &nbsp; &nbsp;The property owner will be liable for any fines incurred as a result of violations of this section.<br><br>E. &nbsp; &nbsp; If a property owner receives a fine for violation of this section, it is the property owner&rsquo;s responsibility to remove all snow and ice placed within the public way immediately. &nbsp;If the property owner fails to address the violation within 7 days, a second fine for violation will be issued in accordance with subsection F hereunder. Any additional violations will be issued in seven (7) day increments in accordance with subsection F hereunder until the violation is addressed.</span></span></div>
<div><span style="font-size: 12px;"><span style="font-family: Times New Roman,Times,serif,-webkit-standard;"><br>F. &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;A property owner in violation of any provision of this section may be subject to a fine of $100.00. If a second violation occurs within the same fiscal year, a fine of $200.00 may be issued. If three or more violations occur within the same fiscal year, a fine of $300.00 may be issued for each subsequent violation. The provisions of G.L. c. 40, s. 21D may be used to enforce this section.&nbsp;</span></span></div>

<p style="page-break-after: always;"><span style="font-size: 12px;"><span style="font-family: Times New Roman,Times,serif,-webkit-standard;"><strong><u>APPLICABLE ORDINANCE<br></u></strong><br>BE IT ORDAINED BY THE COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF SPRINGFIELD, AS FOLLOWS:<br>Section 5 of Chapter 322, Throwing snow and ice into the streets, of the Code of the City of Springfield, is hereby amended by deleting the section in its entirety and inserting the following:<br><br>A. &nbsp; &nbsp;No person, including but not limited to paid or unpaid contractors, property owners, or tenants, shall throw or put, or cause to be thrown or put, any snow or ice into any street in the City or upon any public way. This section shall apply at all times and is not restricted to specific periods following snow events.<br><br>B. &nbsp; &nbsp; This section shall not apply if the Building Commissioner orders the removal of snow or ice from structures adjacent to the public way and a permit is obtained from the Department of Public Works under the provisions of Chapter 369 of the Acts of 1911.<br><br>C. &nbsp; &nbsp; Any person who removes snow or ice and places the snow or ice around or over fire hydrants, into snow piles that result in dangerous driving, biking or pedestrian sight distance issues, in a manner that narrows on-street parking or drive lanes, or in any other manner that creates a hazard to the general public will be in violation of this section.<br><br>D. &nbsp; &nbsp;The property owner will be liable for any fines incurred as a result of violations of this section.<br><br>E. &nbsp; &nbsp; If a property owner receives a fine for violation of this section, it is the property owner&rsquo;s responsibility to remove all snow and ice placed within the public way immediately. &nbsp;If the property owner fails to address the violation within 7 days, a second fine for violation will be issued in accordance with subsection F hereunder. Any additional violations will be issued in seven (7) day increments in accordance with subsection F hereunder until the violation is addressed.</span></span></p>

<p style="page-break-after: always;"><span style="font-size: 12px;"><span style="font-family: Times New Roman,Times,serif,-webkit-standard;"><br>F. &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;A property owner in violation of any provision of this section may be subject to a fine of $100.00. If a second violation occurs within the same fiscal year, a fine of $200.00 may be issued. If three or more violations occur within the same fiscal year, a fine of $300.00 may be issued for each subsequent violation. The provisions of G.L. c. 40, s. 21D may be used to enforce this section.&nbsp;</span></span></p>
